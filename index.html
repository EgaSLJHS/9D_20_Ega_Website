The Lush Green

Borneo's rainforest is one of the oldest on Earth, filled with life and climate importance. From the tall trees to the various animals, this forest is a important landmark that protects the climate and nature.

What is the Borneo Rainforest?

Hover to reveal

The Borneo rainforest is a tropical forest within Indonesia, Malaysia, to Brunei, making it one of the largest rainforests in Southeast Asia. It is estimated to be over 100 million years old, filled with never-ending vegitation, flowing rivers, and thousands of rare animals.

  • Spans three countries
  • One of the oldest in the world
  • Home to many ecosystems

Why It's So Important

Hover to reveal

The rainforest important for the local communities and the global population. It supports many kinds of life and keeps the planet's weather in balance by:

  • Being one of the most diverse ecosystems
  • Absorbing large amounts of carbon dioxide
  • Produces oxygen and prevents climate change
  • Prevents floods for local communities

Unique Wildlife

Hover to reveal

Borneo's rainforest is called home by many rare and unique species. But, many of them, cannot be found anywhere else. This makes it a important place that needs to be preserved. Some of the flora & fauna found there are:

  • Amazing animals: Orangutans, pygmy elephants, & clouded leopards
  • Birds & insects: Hornbills, beatiful butterflies and beetles
  • Unique plant life: Dipterocarp trees, orchids, rafflesia flowers, carnivorous pitcher plants

Protecting it means we protect the lives of the animals that call this place home.

How It Supports the Planet

  • Climate control: Trees absorb carbon dioxide and store it in their trunks/roots, reducing greenhouse gases which slows global warming.
  • Regulates rain: Various plants releases water vapor into the air, which helps form clouds and maintains consistent rainfall.
  • Flood preventation: Tree roots absorb and hold large amounts of water, slowing down the runoff of rain water and prevents rivers from overflowing during storms.
  • Soil protection: Roots attach the soil and the forest's canopy reduces the impact of rainfall, preventing soil from being washed away which keeps the land stable.

A Forest In Danger

Despite its countless importance, Borneo's rainforest is in danger. Lareg areas are being cleared for:

  • Deforestation for palm oil expansion
  • Illegal logging activities
  • Habitat loss and declining wildlife
